Essential Skills Overview

  • Grip: Use either shakehand or penhold. Ensure fingers are relaxed and the paddle feels like an extension of the hand.
  • Forehand: Rotate your waist and use your whole body to generate power. Follow through in the direction you want the ball to go.
  • Backhand: Keep your elbow in front of your body, brush the ball with control, and shift weight slightly forward.
  • Serve: Toss the ball vertically and hit from behind the baseline. Mix spin and placement for strategic advantage.
  • Backspin / Chop: Slice under the ball with a downward motion. Often used defensively to force errors.

Match Rules Summary

Singles Rules

  • Matches are usually best of 1 or best of 3 in school, but official matches are best of 5 or 7 games.
  • Each game is played to 11 points, with at least a 2-point lead to win (e.g. 11–9, 14–12).
  • Players alternate serves every 2 points.
  • If the score reaches 10–10, players alternate serve every point.
  • Players switch sides after each game, and during the final game when one player reaches 5 points (only for best-of-3, 5, or 7 formats).
  • The ball must be tossed vertically at least 16 cm and remain visible during the serve.

Doubles Rules

  • Doubles follows the same scoring and game rules as singles unless otherwise stated.
  • Each pair must alternate hits; the same player cannot hit two consecutive balls.
  • The serve must go diagonally from the right half of the server’s court to the right half of the receiver’s court.
  • Serving order is fixed: Server A → Receiver X → Partner A2 → Partner X2 (and continues rotating).
  • Partners switch roles after each game, and teams switch sides like in singles.
